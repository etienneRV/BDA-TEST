BDA Readiness Assessment Tool

Instructions: Answer each question in the categories below.

Technology

  1. Are there sufficient data storage capabilities to handle increasing data volumes?
  2. Can your infrastructure process large datasets in real-time or near real-time?
  3. How often do you face downtime or performance issues related to infrastructure?
  4. Is your IT infrastructure generally sufficient to support your digital initiatives?
  5. Are data transfers between legacy systems and newer systems automated?
  6. Are legacy systems fully compatible with modern data platforms and software?

Skills

  1. Do you face challenges in recruiting or retaining skilled data professionals?
  2. Are there ongoing training programs to develop data-related skills among staff?
  3. How many employees are proficient in data analysis (machine learning, AI, other big data tools)?
  4. How many employees are proficient in data engineering (ETL processes, data cleaning, etc)?

Data

  1. How frequently do you encounter data errors, inconsistencies, or missing values?
  2. Are there standard data quality checks or procedures in place?
  3. How many independent data storage systems are used across departments?
  4. Are there initiatives to unify or integrate data systems?
  5. Are data extraction, transformation, and loading (ETL) processes automated?
  6. How much manual intervention is required for data consolidation?
  7. Are metadata (e.g., data source, timestamp, data owner) consistently maintained?

Investment

  1. Have you identified specific cost savings, revenue increases, or efficiency improvements from BDA projects?
  2. Are BDA projects monitored for ROI at regular intervals?
  3. How clear are the business objectives linked to BDA projects?

Culture

  1. Have senior executives committed to support the BDA project?
  2. Are employees and departments generally receptive to adopting new technologies?
  3. Are change management strategies (e.g., training, communication) in place to reduce resistance?
  4. Are departments reluctant to share data due to competing objectives or priorities?
  5. Are there clear policies and incentives to encourage cross-departmental collaboration?
  6. Are there stakeholders within your organization prioritize projects for their prestige rather than strategic value?
  7. Does your organization face challenges due to union opposition when implementing new technologies?

Governance

  1. Does your organization have compliance officers or legal teams focused on data protection?
  2. Are there specific regulatory requirements that limit data collection, storage, or processing?

Collaboration

  1. Are you collaborating with other stakeholders (e.g. vendors, customers, etc) to optimise operations?
